Abstract

The domain for our application is education and economic status in Ireland. Applicattion Domain back to ToC

The domain for our application is education and economic status in Ireland.
We have aggregated data such as education level per county, earnings per county, and the number of students per county.
We chose this domain as we are trying to discover if there is an obvious relationship between the level of education in a particular county and its overall economic status.
Are they directly correlated ? Does education = wealth ? Are there more external factors involved ?

R2RML mappings back to ToC

Questions: back to ToC

Q1. Out of the county with the highest number of bachelor degrees, what is the mean earnings?
Q2. What county has the lowest number of third level degrees and what age on average does their education cease?
Q3. What county has the lowest earning and what age on average does their education cease?
Q4. Does the county with highest number of ceased education earn most in 2011?
Q5. Does the county with the lowest secondary school dropout rate earn the most money on average?
Q6. Does the county with the lowest average age education ceased earn the least?
Q7. Does the county with the highest average age for ceasing education earn the most?
Q8. For the county with the most postgraduate degree holders, what was the mean earnings in 2018?
Q9. For the county with the highest percentage of PHD degrees, what's the mean earning?
Q10. In 2016, the county with the lowest mean earning, what age on average do they finish their education ?

Design Descriptions back to ToC

Each data will be mapped to an instance of a subclass of data point.
These objects will reference other classes as attribute.
This allow data points to be searched structurally.
All objects are connected in some way, allow easy enumeration without too much effort as entire graph is connected with a common root.
